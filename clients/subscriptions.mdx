A client's subscription controls whether their advertising profile is publicly visible and actively running on the WordPress site. Exotic CRM surfaces subscription state through a set of badges on the client detail page and provides tools to deactivate subscriptions, assess profile completeness, and track retention risk — all without switching to WordPress.

## Subscription status badges

The **Overview** tab of each client detail page shows a subscription status badge in the profile summary header. The badge reflects the client's current billing and visibility state.

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Active** | The client has a live deal and their profile is publicly visible (`publish` status, no payment block). |
| **Expired** | The client's subscription period has ended. Their profile may still be visible on WordPress, but the deal is no longer active in the CRM. |
| **No subscription** | The client has no deal history in this market. They may have been created manually or synced from WordPress without a corresponding deal. |
| **Private** | The profile exists on WordPress but is set to private — not publicly visible regardless of billing state. |
| **Needs payment** | The WordPress site has flagged this profile as requiring payment before it will be shown again. |

<Note>
  Subscription status is derived from both the client's CRM deal history and the current state of their WordPress profile. If a change is made directly on the WordPress site, run a sync from the client detail page to bring the CRM up to date.
</Note>

## How to deactivate a client subscription

Deactivation sets the client's WordPress profile to private and clears the active subscription in the CRM. Use this when a client has not renewed, requests removal, or violates platform terms.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the client detail page">
    Navigate to **Clients**, find the client, and click their name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Deactivate subscription">
    The **Deactivate subscription** button appears in the **Overview** tab when the client has an active subscription. Click it to open the deactivation dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a reason code">
    Choose the reason that best describes why you are deactivating:

    - **Non-renewal** — the subscription period ended without payment
    - **Client request** — the client asked to be removed
    - **Duplicate** — this profile duplicates another active profile
    - **Policy violation** — the profile violates platform rules
    - **Other** — any other reason (add a note in the reason field)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add reason notes (optional)">
    Type any additional context in the reason notes field. This is stored in the audit log and timeline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notify the client (optional)">
    Enable **Notify client** to send an SMS to the client's registered phone number informing them of the deactivation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm deactivation">
    Click **Deactivate**. The CRM calls the WordPress sync plugin to update the profile status, clears the active deal flags, and records the action in the audit log and client timeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Deactivation is applied to WordPress immediately. The client's profile will no longer appear on the public advertising site once the change propagates. This action cannot be undone from the CRM — to reactivate the profile, you must create a new deal and activate it.
</Warning>

## Profile completeness score

The completeness score gives you a quick read on how fully populated a client's WordPress profile is. A low score typically means missing images, an incomplete bio, or absent contact details — all factors that reduce a profile's effectiveness on the public site.

The score is shown on the **Overview** tab below the subscription summary. It is computed from a set of weighted profile fields pulled from WordPress. The score is fetched separately from the main client record, so it loads slightly after the page renders.

Common completeness gaps and how to fix them:

| Gap | Resolution |
|-----|-----------|
| No profile images | Upload images from the **Media** section of the Edit Profile tab |
| Bio is empty | Fill in the bio from the **Personal** section of Edit Profile |
| Phone not set | Update the phone number in the **Contact** section |
| City not set | Set the city in the **Contact** section |

## Profile health issues

The **Profile Health** tab on the client detail page surfaces issues that cannot be resolved automatically — such as broken WordPress links, duplicate profiles, or mismatched phone numbers.

Each health issue includes:

- A **description** of what is wrong
- A **severity** indicator (warning or critical)
- An **action** you can take to resolve it

### Resolving health issues

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Profile Health tab">
    Navigate to the client detail page and click **Profile Health**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the issues list">
    Each issue is listed with a description and recommended action. Critical issues (shown in red) should be resolved before attempting a renewal or reactivation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take the recommended action">
    Depending on the issue type, resolution may involve:
    - Clicking a **Resolve** or **Fix** button that applies an automated correction
    - Navigating to Edit Profile to update a specific field
    - Using **Repair WP link** to reconnect a broken WordPress association
    - Merging or deleting a duplicate record
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm resolution">
    After acting, click **Mark resolved** (if available) to dismiss the issue from the panel. The health endpoint is re-queried automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Retention bands

Exotic CRM assigns every client a **retention band** based on a computed risk score. The score takes into account subscription age, payment history, online activity, and profile completeness. Bands update automatically as the underlying data changes.

| Band | Description | Recommended action |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Stable** | Low churn risk. Client is active and engaged. | Routine check-ins only. |
| **Watchlist** | Moderate risk. Early warning signs present. | Review subscription expiry and confirm next renewal. |
| **Needs Attention** | Elevated risk. Client is overdue for contact or showing declining activity. | Proactive outreach — call or send a renewal reminder. |
| **Critical** | High churn risk or already inactive. Immediate attention required. | Escalate to a senior agent; attempt immediate contact. |

The band is displayed in both the clients list (Retention column) and the client detail Overview tab.

<Tip>
  Use the **Retention watch** metric card on the Clients list page to instantly filter all clients in the Watchlist, Needs Attention, and Critical bands at once.
</Tip>

## Retention insight panel

The **Overview** tab includes a retention insight panel that shows:

- The client's current **retention score** (lower is better — the score represents churn risk)
- The computed **band** with a colour-coded indicator
- A **primary behaviour tag** describing the dominant risk pattern (e.g. "low activity", "near expiry", "payment gap")
- A **score history chart** showing how the score has moved over the past 90 days

The insight panel is refreshed each time you open the Overview tab, with a short cache window to avoid redundant computation.
