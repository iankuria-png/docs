Leads represent prospective advertising clients before they become paying subscribers. Every lead lives in a specific market (platform) and moves through a defined status lifecycle as your sales team qualifies and contacts them. Once a lead is ready to subscribe, you convert it into a full client record — at which point Exotic CRM links the lead to the new client and can automatically create a deal.

## What is a lead?

A lead is a prospect record that belongs to one market. It stores contact details (name, phone, email), tracks which agent owns it, records the source of the enquiry, and carries a status that reflects how far through the sales process the lead has progressed.

Each lead has a **source** that tells you where it came from:

| Source | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `registration` | Self-registered via the platform |
| `referral` | Referred by an existing client |
| `outbound` | Added manually by the sales team |
| `import` | Imported via CSV or the web scraper |

## Lead status lifecycle

Leads move through five statuses from first contact to outcome.

<Steps>
  <Step title="New">
    The lead has been added but no one has reached out yet. All freshly created or imported leads start here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contacted">
    An agent has made first contact with the prospect. The `first_contact_at` and `last_contact_at` timestamps are recorded automatically when you move a lead to this stage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Qualified">
    The prospect has expressed genuine interest and meets your criteria for a paid subscription. `last_contact_at` is updated again at this stage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Converted">
    The lead has signed up and is now a client. Exotic CRM links the lead to its corresponding client record. You cannot mark a lead as converted without a linked client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Lost">
    The prospect declined or went cold. The lead record is kept for reporting but removed from active pipeline views.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Archiving a lead is separate from marking it **Lost**. An archived lead is hidden from all default list views but is never deleted. Use archive when a lead is a duplicate or otherwise irrelevant, rather than a genuine sales outcome.
</Note>

## Creating a lead manually

Use the **New Lead** button on the Leads page to add a single prospect. You need to provide at minimum a market (platform) and the prospect's name.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the create dialog">
    Click **New Lead** at the top of the Leads page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the prospect details">
    Enter the lead name, phone number (in international format), email address, and the market the lead belongs to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign an agent (optional)">
    Pick a sales agent from the **Assign to** dropdown. If you leave this blank, Exotic CRM attempts to auto-assign based on the agent's market workload.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the lead">
    Click **Create Lead**. The lead appears in the list with status **New**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Phone numbers are normalised automatically using the market's country prefix. You can enter a local number (e.g. `0712 345 678`) and Exotic CRM will store the correct international format.
</Tip>

## Importing leads from a CSV file

You can import up to 500 leads at a time by uploading a CSV file. This is useful when migrating prospects from a spreadsheet or another system.

**CSV column format:**

| Column | Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `name` | Yes | The lead's display name |
| `phone` | No | Local or international format |
| `email` | No | Valid email address |
| `source` | No | `registration`, `referral`, `outbound`, or `import` (defaults to `outbound`) |
| `status` | No | One of the five statuses (defaults to `new`) |
| `assigned_to` | No | Agent user ID |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the CSV import dialog">
    Click **Import CSV** on the Leads page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your market">
    All imported leads are assigned to the market you choose here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload the file">
    Select a `.csv` file from your computer. If your file has a header row, leave the **Has header** toggle on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the results">
    After upload, Exotic CRM reports how many leads were created and lists any rows that failed validation so you can correct and re-upload them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The CSV import limit is **500 rows per upload**. If your file exceeds this, split it into smaller batches before uploading.
</Warning>

## Scraping leads from external sites

The built-in web scraper lets you pull prospect contact details directly from a competitor directory or listing page without leaving Exotic CRM.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Scrape a single entry">
    Use **Scrape Entry** when you have a specific profile URL you want to capture as a lead.

    1. Click **Scrape Entry** on the Leads page.
    2. Paste the profile URL into the **Source URL** field.
    3. Optionally provide a name, phone, and email to supplement what the scraper extracts.
    4. Click **Create**. Exotic CRM derives a lead name from the URL path if you leave the name blank.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Bulk scrape preview">
    Use **Scrape Preview** to pull a batch of prospects from a listing page before committing them to the database.

    1. Click **Scrape** on the Leads page to open the scrape modal.
    2. Choose a preset website or enter a custom listing URL.
    3. Set the maximum number of candidates to fetch (up to 250).
    4. Click **Preview** to see the results before they are saved.
    5. Review the candidate list and quality indicators, then click **Import** to commit the leads. The preview session expires after 30 minutes.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  The scraper respects each site's `robots.txt` rules. If a site blocks automated access, the preview will return a blocked status and no leads will be imported.
</Note>

## Assigning a lead to an agent

Every lead can be assigned to one sales agent at a time. Exotic CRM uses the agent's market access and workload to select the best fit automatically, but you can override this manually.

To reassign a lead, open the row action menu on the Leads page and choose **Assign**. Select the agent and confirm. The previous and new assignees are recorded in the lead's timeline.

<Tip>
  If no eligible active agent is found for the market, the reassignment will fail with an error. Make sure at least one agent has access to that market before assigning.
</Tip>

## Archiving and deleting leads

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Archive a lead" icon="archive">
    Archiving hides the lead from default list views without permanently deleting it. Use this for duplicates or leads that are no longer relevant. Archived leads are recoverable by enabling **Include archived** in the list filters.

    To archive: open the row action menu and choose **Archive**. You must provide a reason.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Delete a lead" icon="trash">
    Deletion is permanent. The lead record and its timeline events are removed. Only delete a lead when you are certain the data is not needed for reporting.

    To delete: open the row action menu and choose **Delete**. You must provide a reason, which is recorded in the audit log before deletion.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  You cannot recover a deleted lead. If you are unsure, archive the lead instead.
</Warning>
