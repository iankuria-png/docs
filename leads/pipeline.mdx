The pipeline gives you a live count of how many leads sit at each stage of the sales funnel for a given market. From here you can advance individual leads, match them against clients who are already in the system, and trigger conversion — turning a qualified prospect into a subscribed client and automatically setting up their first deal.

## The pipeline view

The pipeline view (`GET /api/crm/leads/pipeline`) returns stage counts across all five statuses — **new**, **contacted**, **qualified**, **converted**, and **lost** — for every lead that is not archived. Counts are scoped to the markets you have access to, so each agent only sees their own markets.

Use the market filter on the Leads page to narrow the pipeline to a single platform. The counts update in real time as you advance leads through the stages.

## Moving a lead between stages

You advance a lead by updating its status. Each status change is recorded in the lead's timeline, including the before and after values and who made the change.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lead action menu">
    On the Leads page, find the lead you want to advance and click the action menu in its row.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the next stage">
    Select **Move to [next stage]** from the menu. Exotic CRM prevents you from jumping straight to **Converted** from here — use the dedicated convert action for that.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The lead's status updates immediately and the `last_contact_at` timestamp is refreshed when moving to **Contacted** or **Qualified**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Moving a lead to **Converted** requires a linked client record. If you use the status update action and provide `converted_client_id`, Exotic CRM validates that the client belongs to the same market as the lead before accepting the change.
</Note>

## Lead reconciliation

Reconciliation is the process of matching a lead to an existing client record that is already in Exotic CRM. This happens when a prospect registered directly on the platform before your sales team added them as a lead, or when a CSV import creates leads that overlap with known clients.

Exotic CRM checks for matches automatically using these signals, in priority order:

1. A previously confirmed link on the lead
2. Matching WordPress profile ID
3. Matching WordPress user account
4. Matching phone number — only when exactly one client in the market matches

The match confidence is shown on each lead:

| Confidence | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `confirmed` | Manually verified — the lead is directly linked to the client |
| `high` | WordPress post or user ID matched |
| `medium` | Phone number matched a single client |
| `low` | Indirect or ambiguous match |

### Reconciling a single lead

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the reconcile dialog">
    On the lead's row action menu, choose **Reconcile**. The dialog shows any automatically detected client match and its confidence level.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a reconciliation action">
    | Action | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | `link` | Links the lead to the client without changing the lead's status |
    | `convert` | Links the lead and sets its status to **Converted** |
    | `archive` | Links the lead and archives it (use when the lead is a duplicate) |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide a reason and confirm">
    Enter a reason for the reconciliation — this is required and recorded in the audit log.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Batch reconciliation

Batch reconciliation processes multiple leads at once, automatically finding their matching client records and applying the same action to all of them.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select leads for batch reconciliation">
    Use the checkboxes on the Leads page to select specific leads, or leave the selection empty to let Exotic CRM auto-select all unarchived leads that have a matching phone number in the clients table.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open batch reconcile">
    Click **Batch Reconcile** from the bulk actions toolbar. Choose **link**, **convert**, or **archive** as the action.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review results">
    Exotic CRM reports how many leads were processed, linked, converted, archived, and failed. Failures occur when no matching client can be resolved for a lead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Batch reconciliation is limited to **500 leads per request**. If your selection is larger, narrow it down by filtering on a specific market or status first.
</Warning>

## Converting a lead to a client

Conversion creates a brand-new client record from the lead's data and permanently links the two together. Use this when the prospect has not previously registered on the platform and does not yet have a client record in Exotic CRM.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the convert dialog">
    On the lead's row action menu, choose **Convert to Client**. The dialog pre-fills from the lead's stored contact details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and complete the client profile">
    Confirm or edit the name, phone number, email address, city, and initial profile status. You can also assign the new client to a specific agent at this point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the conversion">
    Click **Convert**. Exotic CRM checks for existing clients with the same phone number or email. If a likely duplicate is found, you will see a conflict response — review the suggested existing client before proceeding.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If an existing client is found with the same phone or email, the conversion returns a `409 Conflict` response with details of the matched client. You should use **Reconcile** to link the lead to that existing client instead of creating a duplicate.
</Note>

## What happens after conversion

When a lead is successfully converted:

1. A new client record is created and synced to the platform.
2. The lead's status is set to **Converted** and its `converted_client_id` is stored.
3. A timeline event is recorded on both the lead and the new client.
4. You can immediately create a deal for the new client from the client detail page or the Deals page.

<Tip>
  After converting a lead, navigate to the new client's profile to review their subscription options and create their first deal. The client profile inherits the lead's assigned agent by default.
</Tip>
