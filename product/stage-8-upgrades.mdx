## What the customer sees

An escort on a lower tier (e.g. Basic or Featured) may want to upgrade to a higher-visibility package (e.g. Premium or VIP) after seeing the difference in client enquiry volume. Upgrades are not self-service — the escort contacts their sales agent or the Exotic CS team, who process the upgrade through the CRM.

Once an upgrade is processed, the escort's profile re-provisions at the new tier on WordPress — their listing moves to the higher position in search results and gains any additional features of the new package.

---

## Under the hood

| What happens | Code reference |
|---|---|
| Staff updates the deal's product and/or duration | `DealController::update()` — `app/Http/Controllers/CRM/DealController.php:87` — updates `product_id`, `duration`, `status` |
| Lifecycle re-classification triggered | `SubscriptionLifecycleService::resolveForDeal()` — determines if this is a new subscription or renewal |
| Profile reprovisioned at new tier on WordPress | `SubscriptionProvisioningService` — same activation path as Stage 5d |
| Discount support | `deals.discount_percentage`, `deals.discount_approved_by` — discounts above threshold require approval |
| Product tiers | `products.tier` — links to `ProductPrice` (weekly / bi-weekly / monthly pricing) |
