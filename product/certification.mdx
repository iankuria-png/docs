## Part 1 — Customer journey knowledge

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Q1: List the nine stages of the Exotic escort lifecycle in order.">
    **Answer:**
    1. Discover
    2. Register & account creation
    3. Profile setup & media upload
    4. Verification
    5a. Subscription selection
    5b. Payment initiation
    5c. Payment confirmation
    5d. Profile activation
    6. Customer contact
    7. Renewal
    8. Upgrades
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q2: What is the SINGLE fastest way to locate a payment record in the CRM?">
    **Answer:** Search by the **M-Pesa transaction code** (e.g. `RFX4K2T8W5`) from the escort's confirmation SMS. CRM → Payments → search bar → paste the code.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q3: An escort's profile was live yesterday but is private today and they didn't request deactivation. Name THREE possible causes.">
    **Answer (any three):**
    - Subscription expired (`deals.expires_at` passed)
    - M-Pesa payment was reversed (`payments.status = 'reversed'`)
    - Staff manually deactivated the subscription
    - Amount mismatch placed the payment in review and provisioning was not completed
    - WordPress sync failure left the profile in a stale state
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q4: Which payment method should CS suggest when an escort wants to pay from a phone number different from their registered M-Pesa number?">
    **Answer:** **Payment link** (method = `link` in CRM → Deals → Activate). The link can be opened and paid from any M-Pesa number or card, not just the registered one.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q5: What three contact channels can a visitor use to contact an escort on the public Exotic site?">
    **Answer:** WhatsApp (primary), phone call/reveal, SMS, Viber. Telegram is coming soon.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Part 2 — Department knowledge

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Q6 (Sales & CS): Name the three key points used to handle the objection 'Why should I pay — there are free listing sites?'">
    **Answer:**
    1. **Lead quality** — Exotic attracts serious, paying clients. Free sites are full of time-wasters.
    2. **Visibility** — Paid profiles rank higher on Google and within the site. Free listings are buried.
    3. **Support** — Dedicated CS, renewal reminders, and account management that free sites don't offer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q7 (Finance): What does 'wallet_funding_settlement_review' mean, and what action does Finance take?">
    **Answer:** The payment amount received differs from the expected subscription price by more than 5%. The CRM holds the payment for Finance to manually review. Finance checks whether the difference is acceptable (e.g. due to a discount), then confirms or rejects the match. If confirmed, provisioning proceeds automatically.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q8 (Finance): Name the three CS complaints identified as most common, in any order.">
    **Answer:**
    1. "I paid but my profile isn't live"
    2. "I can't log in to my account"
    3. "I'm not getting any client contacts"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q9 (IT): A batch of webhook callbacks is being rejected with status 401. What is the cause and fix?">
    **Answer:** **Cause:** HMAC signature mismatch — the webhook secret in the CRM doesn't match the secret configured in KopoKopo. **Fix:** Log into KopoKopo dashboard, copy the current webhook secret, update it in CRM → Settings → Integrations → [market] → KopoKopo webhook secret. Then manually re-activate any deals that missed activation during the failure window.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q10 (SEO & IT): What determines which package tier position an escort appears at in search results?">
    **Answer:** The `products.tier` field on the deal's linked product. Higher tiers (VIP, VVIP) appear above lower tiers (Featured, Basic) in search results. This is configured per market in Settings → Integrations → Packages.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Part 3 — Scenario test

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Scenario A: The 'I paid but nothing happened' call">
    **Situation:** An escort calls in. She paid KES 5,000 via M-Pesa 2 hours ago. She has the M-Pesa SMS with code `QWE9876543`. Her profile is still private.

    **Write your exact step-by-step response:**

    **Model answer:**
    1. Note the transaction code: `QWE9876543`
    2. CRM → Payments → search `QWE9876543`
    3. Diagnose based on what you find:
       - **Payment not found:** Escalate to IT — webhook may not have arrived. Ask escort to wait and call back in 15 minutes.
       - **`initiated` or `pending`:** Payment not confirmed yet. Ask her to check her M-Pesa messages — did she receive a confirmation? If yes, escalate to IT (callback may be delayed). If no, she may not have completed the PIN entry — retry via payment link.
       - **`completed` + deal `active`:** Her profile should be live. Ask her to hard-refresh her browser and search for her name on the site directly.
       - **`completed` + deal `pending`:** Provisioning is stuck. Escalate to IT with deal ID and payment ID.
       - **`manual_review`:** Finance needs to confirm the payment match first — inform Finance and give the escort an ETA of 2–4 hours.
       - **`reversed`:** M-Pesa reversed her payment. Inform her clearly and empathetically; she needs to pay again. Check if there's a system issue causing reversals.
    4. Whatever the outcome: create a note in her CRM timeline documenting the complaint and your action.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Scenario B: New team member onboarding quiz">
    **Situation:** A new sales agent joins the team. They ask you: "Walk me through what happens from the moment a new escort decides to pay, to the moment their profile is live."

    **Give the answer in plain language (no jargon):**

    **Model answer:**
    "The escort goes to the Exotic website and clicks the Subscribe button on their profile. They see the available packages — Basic, Featured, Premium, VIP, VVIP — and pick the one they want along with how long they want to subscribe for (weekly or monthly).

    They click Pay. If they're using M-Pesa, they enter their phone number and get a prompt on their phone asking for their M-Pesa PIN. They enter the PIN and get an M-Pesa confirmation SMS — that's their proof the payment went through.

    Meanwhile, our system receives a notification from M-Pesa confirming the payment. It matches the payment to the escort's account, creates a subscription deal, and tells our WordPress site to publish their profile. The whole thing takes about 30 to 60 seconds.

    The escort's checkout page updates to say 'Your profile is now live!' and they can click through to see their listing on the site.

    If anything goes wrong at any of those steps — payment not confirmed, profile not activated, wrong phone number — that's when CS steps in using the CRM and the M-Pesa transaction code to diagnose and fix the issue."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
