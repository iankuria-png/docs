## Part 1 — Customer journey knowledge

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Q1: List all the stages of the Exotic escort lifecycle in order.">
    **Answer:**

    1. Discover
    2. Register & account creation
    3. Profile setup & media upload
    4. Verification
    5. Subscription selection
    6. Payment initiation
    7. Payment confirmation
    8. Profile activation
    9. Customer contact
    10. Renewal
    11. Upgrades
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q2: What is the SINGLE fastest way to locate a payment record in the CRM?">
    **Answer:** Search by the **M-Pesa transaction code** (e.g. `RFX4K2T8W5`) from the escort's confirmation SMS. CRM → Payments → search bar → paste the code.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q3: An escort's profile was live yesterday but is private today and they didn't request deactivation. Name THREE possible causes.">
    **Answer (any three):**

    - Subscription expired (`deals.expires_at` passed)
    - M-Pesa payment was reversed (`payments.status = 'reversed'`)
    - Staff manually deactivated the subscription
    - Amount mismatch placed payment in review and provisioning was not completed
    - WordPress sync failure left the profile in a stale state
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q4: Which payment method should CS suggest when an escort wants to pay from a different phone number than their registered M-Pesa number?">
    **Answer:** **Payment link** (method = `link` in CRM → Deals → Activate). The link can be opened and paid from any M-Pesa number or card — not just the registered one.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q5: What contact channels can a visitor use to reach an escort on the Exotic site?">
    **Answer:** WhatsApp (primary), phone call/reveal, SMS, and Viber. **Exotic Chat** is separate from the public profile contact buttons — it is being built to help Exotic maintain more consistent communication with escorts.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Part 2 — Department knowledge

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Q6 (Sales & CS): Name the three key points used to handle the objection 'Why should I pay — there are free listing sites?'">
    **Answer:**

    1. **Lead quality** — Exotic attracts serious, paying clients. Free sites are full of time-wasters.
    2. **Visibility** — Paid profiles rank higher on Google and within the site. Free listings are buried.
    3. **Support** — Dedicated CS, renewal reminders, and account management that free sites don't offer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q7 (Finance): What does 'wallet_funding_settlement_review' mean, and what action does Finance take?">
    **Answer:** The payment amount received differs from the expected subscription price by more than 5%. The CRM holds the payment for Finance to manually review. Finance checks whether the difference is acceptable (e.g. due to a discount), then confirms or rejects the match. If confirmed, provisioning proceeds automatically.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q8 (Commercial leadership): Who owns package tiers, pricing, and the decision to open new markets?">
    **Answer:** Package tiers, features, and pricing are owned by the **CS head for each market or region** and require approval from the **Head of CS/Sales**. The decision to open a new African market sits with the **CEO**, guided by Sales input, R&D/Product input, and market research. IT Infrastructure executes the technical rollout after the decision is approved.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q9 (R&D/Product): A batch of webhook callbacks is being rejected with status 401. What is the cause and fix?">
    **Answer:** **Cause:** HMAC signature mismatch — the webhook secret in the CRM doesn't match the secret configured in KopoKopo. **Fix:** Log into KopoKopo dashboard → copy current webhook secret → CRM → Settings → Integrations → [market] → update KopoKopo webhook secret. Then manually re-activate any deals that missed activation during the failure window.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Q10 (SEO & Marketing): What determines which package tier position an escort appears at in search results?">
    **Answer:** The `products.tier` field on the deal's linked product. Higher tiers (VIP, VVIP) appear above lower tiers (Featured, Basic) in all search results. Commercial tier and pricing decisions come from CS leadership, while the approved configuration is applied in the platform by R&D/Product with IT support where needed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Part 3 — Scenario test

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Scenario A: The 'I paid but nothing happened' call">
    **Situation:** An escort calls in. She paid KES 5,000 via M-Pesa 2 hours ago. She has the M-Pesa SMS with code `QWE9876543`. Her profile is still private.

    **Walk through your exact step-by-step response:**

    **Model answer:**

    1. Note the transaction code: `QWE9876543`
    2. CRM → Payments → search `QWE9876543`
    3. Diagnose based on what you find:
       - **Payment not found:** Escalate to R&D/Product — webhook may not have arrived. Involve IT Infrastructure if there are environment or dashboard-access issues. Ask escort to wait and call back in 15 minutes.
       - **`initiated` or `pending`:** Payment not confirmed yet. Ask her to check her M-Pesa messages — did she receive a confirmation SMS? If yes, escalate to R&D/Product (callback delayed). If no, she may not have completed PIN entry — retry via payment link.
       - **`completed` + deal `active`:** Her profile should be live. Ask her to hard-refresh her browser and search for her name directly on the site.
       - **`completed` + deal `pending`:** Provisioning is stuck. Check whether the payment still needs a client match; if not, escalate to R&D/Product with deal ID and payment ID.
        - **`manual_review`:** Sales & CS market operations should confirm the client match quickly; involve Finance if the amount or receipt is unclear.
        - **`reversed`:** M-Pesa reversed her payment. Inform her clearly and empathetically — she needs to pay again.
    4. Whatever the outcome: create a timeline note on her CRM record documenting the complaint and your action.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Scenario B: New team member onboarding question">
    **Situation:** A new sales agent joins the team. They ask: "Walk me through what happens from the moment an escort decides to pay, to the moment their profile is live."

    **Give the answer in plain language:**

    **Model answer:** "The escort goes to the Exotic website and clicks Subscribe on their profile. They see the available packages — Basic, Featured, Premium, VIP, VVIP — and pick the one they want, plus the billing period (weekly or monthly).

    They click Pay. If they're using M-Pesa, they enter their phone number and get a prompt on their phone asking for their PIN. They enter the PIN and receive an M-Pesa confirmation SMS — that's their proof of payment.

    Meanwhile, our system receives a notification from M-Pesa. It matches the payment to the escort's account, creates a subscription deal, and tells our WordPress site to publish their profile. The whole thing takes about 30 to 60 seconds.

    The checkout page updates to say 'Your profile is now live!' — they can click through to see their listing.

    If anything goes wrong — payment not confirmed, profile not activated, wrong phone number — that's when CS steps in using the CRM and the M-Pesa transaction code to diagnose and fix the issue."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Scenario C: Overnight payment pile-up (Finance / CS)">
    **Situation:** It's Monday morning. You find 12 payments in the CRM recovery queue — all showing `completed` but with no linked deals. They all arrived over the weekend.

    **Walk through what you do:**

    **Model answer:**

    1. Run the M-Pesa review panel: CRM → Payments → M-Pesa review — this surfaces exactly these unmatched payments
    2. For each unmatched payment: check the phone number on the payment → search for a matching client in CRM → Clients
    3. If a match is found: Sales & CS market operations confirms the payment match → provisioning triggers automatically → profile goes live
    4. If no match: check if the escort registered with a different number; create the client record if needed; then match
    5. Contact each affected escort to confirm their profile is now live and apologise for the delay
    6. Flag the batch to R&D/Product — a spike of 12 `client_unresolved` payments over a weekend warrants investigation into the auto-matching logic
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Part 4 — "Take me through your day"

This section tests whether you can describe a complete working shift as a Sales & CS agent. The best answer shows both the tools you use and the judgment calls you make throughout the day. There's no single right answer — but a strong answer covers all the key areas below.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sales & CS Agent — describe a typical working shift from start to finish">
    **Situation:** It's 8:00 AM on a Tuesday. Walk us through your day as a Sales & CS agent at Exotic.

    **What a strong answer covers:**

    **Morning (8:00–10:00) — Operations check:**

    - Check the **recovery queue**: CRM → Payments → filter Status = Recovery queue. Any `completed` payments not yet matched to a client need immediate attention from market operations.
    - Check the **failed payment queue** and identify customers who still show payment intent, then reach out with the right recovery option or payment link.
    - Check the **message backlog** (WhatsApp/SMS/chat) and prioritise paying customers, receipt-sharing customers, and activation blockers.
    - Check **expired subscriptions** and prioritise high-value tiers for follow-up.
    - Check **at-risk renewals**: CRM → Renewals → Overview → at\_risk bucket. Who expires in the next 7 days? Build your call list.
    - Check whether there are any fresh **"no calls / profile not visible"** complaints that need a visibility review with SEO & Marketing instead of a payment or support response

    **Mid-morning (10:00–11:30) — Outbound sales:**

    - Work through the CRM → Clients list: new sign-ups and overnight registrations that still need conversion help
    - Make outbound calls on the at-risk renewal list — personal calls convert far better than automated reminders
    - For new leads: follow Stage 1 SOP — confirm interest, qualify, guide toward registration
    - Chase daily conversions from every angle: failed payments, new users, expired subscriptions, and warm sign-ups who are already halfway down the funnel

    **Midday (11:30–12:30) — Inbound support:**

    - Respond to WhatsApp messages from escorts with questions or payment issues
    - For any "I paid but profile isn't live": ask for M-Pesa transaction code, search CRM → Payments, diagnose using the Stage 5e failure reference
    - For any "my profile is not visible" or "I did not get calls": confirm profile status, tier, and quality, then compare traffic/indexation context with SEO & Marketing before assuming there is a bug

    **Afternoon (13:30–16:00) — Profile and activation work:**

    - Follow up on any escorts who are in registration/verification/profile setup stages
    - Process upgrade conversations from the morning renewal calls
    - Document any client notes on the CRM as you go

    **End of day (16:00–17:00) — Wrap-up:**

    - Complete any open CRM timeline notes
    - Check the recovery queue one more time — anything that arrived during the day that still needs matching, review, or escalation?
    - Review your contact pipeline: any client/lead/contact that have been in `contacted` status for more than 21 days without progress → move to `lost` with reason, or re-engage
    - Log any product feedback or recurring escort complaints for the weekly team meeting

    **The judgment calls that mark a great agent:**

    - Prioritising the verification queue every morning — an escort waiting for verification is on the edge of dropping off
    - Outbound sales - users who signed up by themselves are halfway through the pipeline - reach out and convert
    - Not pushing for payment before a profile is complete — a disappointed escort who goes live with bad photos churns within a week
    - Knowing which payment failures to fix yourself (F2, F3) vs. escalating to R&D/Product (F5, F6), IT Infrastructure (F10), or Finance (F7, F8)
    - Treating renewal calls as relationship calls, not sales calls — asking "how's it going?" before pitching renewal

    Note: This is a theoretical cover - it is not meant to restrict you to doing this tasks step by step - it is meant to give you a mental guide/map (Operational check → Outbound Sales →Inbound Support→Profile Activation Work) on what needs to be covered during a usual sales operation day to ensure we are moving the needle across. (As we say, kwa ground vitu ni different - optimize and adapt this mental models to help guide your workflow not restrict you)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Finance Officer — describe a weekly reconciliation shift">
    **Situation:** It's 9:00 AM. Walk through a Finance reconciliation shift.

    **What a strong answer covers:**

    **Morning reconciliation:**

    1. CRM → Payments → filter: Status = completed, Date = yesterday → Export to XLSX
    2. Cross-reference each transaction reference with the KopoKopo / M-Pesa settlement report for the same date
    3. Check the **manual review queue**: CRM → Payments → filter: Review state = manual review. Any amount-mismatch or disputed payments need confirmation or rejection today.
    4. Note any `reversed` payments — update the reconciliation log; check if the reversal was M-Pesa's error or fraud-related.
    5. Check the **recovery queue**: any `completed` payments with no linked deal — coordinate with CS to match them to clients.

    **Settlement timing to track:**

    - KopoKopo: same day or T\+1
    - M-Pesa Daraja: T\+1
    - Paystack / Pesapal / PawaPay: per your provider agreement

    **End of day:**

    - Confirm all manual review items from today are actioned (confirmed or rejected)
    - Update the daily reconciliation log

    Discount guardrails are defined commercially inside Sales & CS leadership. Finance should reflect discounted revenue correctly in reporting, but does not own day-to-day discount approval.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
