## What the customer sees

Once an escort decides to join Exotic, they register on the public WordPress site. They fill in a registration form with their display name, phone number, email address, and city. A WordPress account is created for them, along with a profile post (initially in `private` status). They receive login credentials — either by email or via SMS from a staff member.

The escort is now a registered user but not yet listed publicly. Their profile exists but needs photos, bio, and subscription payment before it goes live.

---

## Under the hood

| What happens | Code reference |
|---|---|
| Escort's lead record converted to a client in CRM | `LeadConversionService::convert()` — `app/Services/LeadConversionService.php:61` — creates WP user + post, then creates Client row |
| Client record created with `profile_status = 'private'` | `app/Models/Client.php` |
| Automatic sync from WordPress → CRM | `ClientSyncService::fullSync()` / `deltaSync()` — `app/Services/ClientSyncService.php:14` |
| WordPress sync plugin REST endpoint | `class-client-sync-endpoint.php` in `exotic-crm-sync` plugin |
| Phone deduplication on conversion | Prevents duplicate clients with same phone number |
