## What the customer sees

An escort discovers Exotic through one of three channels — a referral from a friend or colleague already on the platform, an outbound call from a member of the Exotic sales team, or an organic Google search for escort listing sites in their country.

**Referrals** are the highest-converting channel. An escort who hears "I'm on Exotic and I'm getting good clients" from a trusted colleague arrives with strong intent and minimal objections. Referred escorts typically convert faster and have better first-year retention.

**Outbound sales calls** are the highest-volume channel. The sales team proactively calls prospects identified through competitor site scraping, industry contacts, or market intelligence. These calls require the most sales skill — the escort has not expressed prior interest.

**Organic search (SEO)** produces self-service arrivals — escorts who found Exotic on Google and visit the site without any staff interaction. These escorts may register and even start the subscription flow without speaking to anyone. They often need more hand-holding during payment and activation.

## Under the hood

When a prospect is identified, they enter the CRM as a **Lead**. Every lead belongs to a market (country/platform) and moves through a defined lifecycle.

| What happens | Code reference |
|---|---|
| Lead created manually by sales agent | `LeadController` — `app/Http/Controllers/CRM/LeadController.php` |
| Lead imported from competitor scraping | Scraper sources — CRM → Settings → Integrations → Scraper sources |
| SupportBoard chat contact auto-imported as lead | `SupportBoardLeadImportService` — `app/Services/SupportBoardLeadImportService.php` |
| Lead record fields | `app/Models/Lead.php` — name, phone (normalised), email, source, status, assigned agent |
| Phone normalised on creation | Local format (e.g. `0712...`) → international (e.g. `254712...` for Kenya) |
| Lead status lifecycle | `new` → `contacted` → `qualified` → `converted` → `lost` |

## Step-by-step SOP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the prospect">
    Prospects come from three sources:
    - **Referral:** An existing client provides a contact. Log the referral in the CRM lead record under Source = `referral` and note the referring client's name.
    - **Outbound:** Sales agent scrapes or identifies a prospect from a competitor site. Use CRM → Leads → Scrape Entry or bulk Scrape Preview to import.
    - **SEO/inbound:** Escort visits the site and registers. They may appear in the Leads list automatically if they initiated a SupportBoard chat.

    **Screenshot slot:** *CRM Leads list showing new leads from different sources*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create or confirm the lead in CRM">
    If the prospect is not yet in the CRM:
    1. CRM → Leads → New Lead
    2. Enter name, phone (local format is fine — CRM normalises it), email, select market
    3. Set source: `registration`, `referral`, `outbound`, or `import`
    4. Assign to yourself or the relevant agent

    If the prospect already exists (duplicate check): search by phone number first. Do not create duplicates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make first contact">
    Call, WhatsApp, or SMS the prospect. Your goal at this stage is one thing: **confirm interest**. Do not try to close the sale on the first contact.

    Key questions to answer in the first call:
    - Are they currently listed on any other site?
    - What market are they in (city/country)?
    - Do they have a smartphone and M-Pesa / mobile money set up?

    After contact, update the lead status to `contacted` in the CRM.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Qualify the lead">
    A lead is **qualified** when the escort has expressed genuine interest and you've confirmed they can pay (they have mobile money access). Update status to `qualified`.

    Unqualified leads (wrong market, not interested, no mobile money) should be marked `lost` with a reason — don't leave them in `new` indefinitely.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hand off to registration">
    Once qualified, guide the escort to register on the public Exotic site. The lead continues to Stage 2.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I handle an escort who found us on Google and registered without calling us first?">
    Check if they appear in CRM → Clients (synced from WordPress) or CRM → Leads (if they used the chat widget). If they've registered, assign them to an agent and make proactive contact — welcome them, offer to walk them through profile setup, and guide them toward subscription. Self-service registrants often drop off at the payment stage without a nudge.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if a prospect is in a country where Exotic isn't configured yet?">
    Log the lead in the CRM and mark them as `qualified` with a note about the market gap. Escalate to your manager who will flag it to the RD Team. Do not promise a launch date — that's RD's decision.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I log a referral properly?">
    Create the new lead in CRM → Leads → New Lead. Set Source = `referral`. In the notes field, write the referring client's name and CRM client ID. This enables referral attribution in reports.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long should I chase a prospect before marking them Lost?">
    Standard practice: 3 contact attempts over 7 days. If no response after the third attempt, mark `lost` with reason "no response". You can always re-open a lost lead if they contact you later.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Likely cause | Where to look | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prospect doesn't appear in CRM after registration | WordPress → CRM sync hasn't run | CRM → Settings → Integrations → market → Sync clients | Run manual sync; check sync status |
| Duplicate lead records for same prospect | Lead created twice (manual + auto-import) | CRM → Leads → search by phone | Archive the duplicate; keep the one with more information |
| Scraper returning no results for a competitor URL | Site blocks automated access or URL format changed | CRM → Settings → Scraper sources → Run now → check error | Update source URL; check robots.txt compliance |
| Lead assigned to wrong market | Phone prefix mismatch during normalisation | CRM → Leads → lead detail → market field | Manually update the market on the lead record |
